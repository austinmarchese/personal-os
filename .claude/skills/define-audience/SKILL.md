# Define Audience

An interactive interview to define your target audience. Helps you create content that resonates with a specific person, not a vague demographic.

## When to Use

- Starting a content strategy (social media, newsletter, YouTube)
- Launching a product or service
- When content feels generic and you need to sharpen it

## Process

### Step 1: Explain the Goal

> "Let's define who you're creating for. The goal is to get specific enough that you can picture ONE person — not a demographic like '25-35 professionals.'
>
> By the end, you'll have a profile you can reference every time you create something."

### Step 2: Start with Who You Know

Ask:
> "Think of someone who already loves what you do — a customer, follower, or friend who 'gets it.'
>
> Can you describe them? What's their name, what do they do, what are they struggling with?"

If they can't think of someone:
> "That's okay. Let's build a composite. Who do you WANT to help? What problem do they have that you can solve?"

### Step 3: Go Deeper — Problems and Aspirations

Ask:
> "What are the top 3 problems this person is dealing with?"

Then:
> "What are they hoping to achieve? What does success look like for them?"

Then:
> "What have they already tried that didn't work?"

### Step 4: Get Specific — Day in Their Life

Ask:
> "Walk me through their day. When do they feel the problem most? Where are they when they'd encounter your content?"

This helps with:
- Content format (short videos for commute? Long reads for evening?)
- Messaging (what moment are you speaking to?)

### Step 5: Language and Objections

Ask:
> "How would THEY describe their problem? What words would they use?"

Then:
> "What would make them skeptical of you? What objection would they have?"

### Step 7: Save and Confirm

Save to `knowledge/me/audience.md`:

> "Saved your audience profile to `knowledge/me/audience.md`.
>
> From now on, when you create content, I'll reference this to make sure it resonates with [Name].
>
> You can always update this by running `/define-audience` again."

## Tips

- One specific person > a broad demographic
- Use their actual words when possible
- Problems and aspirations are more useful than demographics
- If they serve multiple audiences, create multiple profiles
