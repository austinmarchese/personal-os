# Weekly Review

A guided weekly reflection. Captures what you learned, what worked, and what's next.

## When to Use

- End of week (Friday or Sunday)
- When you feel scattered and need to reset
- Before planning a new week

## Process

### Step 1: Set the Stage

> "Let's do your weekly review. This takes about 10 minutes.
>
> I'll ask you a few questions, capture the key insights, and help you plan next week.
>
> Ready?"

### Step 2: Review What Happened

Ask:
> "What were the highlights of this week? What went well?"

Then:
> "What was frustrating or didn't go as planned?"

Then:
> "What did you learn this week — any insights or realizations?"

### Step 3: Check Progress on Goals

If `knowledge/me/about.md` has goals, reference them:
> "Looking at your goals:
> - [Goal 1]
> - [Goal 2]
>
> How did this week move you toward these?"

If no goals exist:
> "What were you trying to accomplish this week? Did you make progress?"

### Step 4: Capture Learnings

For each learning mentioned, ask:
> "Can you give me the short version I should remember? Something like 'I learned that X because Y.'"

These become entries in `knowledge/lived-experiences/`.

### Step 5: Plan Next Week

Ask:
> "What's the ONE thing that would make next week a success?"

Then:
> "What might get in the way? How can you prevent that?"

Then:
> "Any tasks or commitments you need to remember?"

### Step 6: Save and Summarize

Create a weekly review entry:
- Save learnings to `knowledge/lived-experiences/YYYY-MM-DD-weekly-review.md`

Format:
```markdown
# Weekly Review — [Date Range]

## Highlights
-

## Challenges
-

## Learnings
-

## Progress on Goals
-

## Next Week
- Focus:
- Watch out for:
- Key tasks:
```

Confirm:
> "Saved your weekly review.
>
> **Key takeaways:**
> - [Learning 1]
> - [Learning 2]
>
> **Next week's focus:** [One thing]
>
> Have a great week."

## Tips

- Keep it light — this shouldn't feel like homework
- One good learning is better than five vague ones
- Connect learnings to goals when possible
- If they're rushed, just capture highlights and one learning
